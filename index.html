BACKGROUND

About

Dr. Aji A. Anappara is a Professor in the Department of Physics at the National Institute of Technology Calicut. His research focuses on advanced functional materials, optoelectronic devices, and light–matter interactions, with an emphasis on translating fundamental science into practical technologies and patented innovations.

He completed his Ph.D. under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Alessandro Tredicucci at the NEST (National Enterprise for nanoScience and nanoTechnology), Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa, Italy.

Following his doctoral studies, he was awarded a prestigious fellowship from the Alexander von Humboldt Foundation, where he worked with Prof. Alfred Leitenstorfer and Prof. Rupert Huber at the University of Konstanz.

He joined NIT Calicut in November 2010 as an Assistant Professor and is currently a Professor. He leads the Photonic Materials and Devices Laboratory, working at the interface of nanomaterials, optoelectronics, and ionotronic systems.

His work has resulted in high-impact publications, multiple patents (granted and filed), and externally funded research projects, reflecting a strong synergy between fundamental science and technological innovation.

Research Overview

Our group operates at the interface of nanomaterials science, device physics, and light-driven chemical processes, specializing in low-dimensional material systems for next-generation optoelectronic, ionotronic, and neuromorphic technologies.

Optoelectronics
Self-Powered Broadband Photodetectors
Developing zero-bias, dark-current-free, broadband (UV–Vis–NIR) photodetectors on flexible and unconventional substrates including paper and polymer platforms. Supported by ANRF-ARG funding (2026–2029).
neuromorphic
High-Temperature Photodetectors
Thermally resilient photodetection devices operating reliably from room temperature to ~500°C using hybrid p–n heterojunction architectures, surpassing conventional Si and InGaAs technologies.
Ionotronics
Retinomorphic & Ionotronic Synapses
Bio-inspired retinomorphic photodetectors and opto-ionotronic synaptic devices that emulate human visual response, enabling event-driven sensing, in-sensor processing, and neuromorphic computing.
catalysis
Optofluidics & Analog Computing
Optofluidic platforms with solid-state ionic transport enabling continuous-time hardware-level differentiation and integration — opening paths toward analog computing and unconventional information processing.
2d-materials
Light-Driven Chemical Transformations
Functional nanomaterials for photothermal catalysis, light-induced polymer upcycling, and sustainable chemical transformations — with patented approaches for energy-efficient reaction engineering.
Nanomaterials
Low-Dimensional & Multivalent Systems
Structure–property studies of 2D nanosheets, carbon dots, boron-based nanostructures, carbide nanosheets, and elemental chalcogens. Exploring dimensionality-driven phase transformations and optical properties.
